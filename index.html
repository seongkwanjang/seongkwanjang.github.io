Research

Hysteresis in Soft Robotics

Sep. 2024 – Aug. 2025

Hysteresis in soft pneumatic actuators leads to complex and unpredictable actuations, resulting in difficulties of modeling and control. Previous studies inevitably involve complex hysteresis modeling, additional compensators or controllers for hysteresis compensation, which in turn lead to high computational costs. In this paper, we propose a novel hysteresis compensation approach by combining two counteractive hysteretic components. As this hysteresis compensation approach is only based on the hardware, complex modeling and additional compensators or controllers are unnecessary.

  • Extended prior work (bachelor's thesis) on hysteresis of a Yoshimura origami pneumatic pump into a novel hardware-based hysteresis compensation approach.
  • Proposed a theoretical framework of the approach with mathematical proof and parametric study using the Prandtl-Ishlinskii (P-I) hysteresis model.
  • Analyzed hysteresis compensation using a within the combination of an origami pump and soft pneumatic actuator with validation experiments and P-I model optimized with GA + Nelder-Mead algorithm.
